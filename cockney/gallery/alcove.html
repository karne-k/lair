Set within the alcove is a triptych - two wings hinged and shaped to interlock and cover a large central panel. It stands open and you can see images on both the inner and outer surfaces...

For those who don't know, a triptych is a three paneled church alterpiece. This monstrosity (total painted surface is 3 squ. metres!) was the final piece for my AS level art exam and had to be completed in 20 hours.. That makes it easily the largest and most frantic picture I've ever done.

The title (fixed by the examiner) was Transition, which was basically another 'paint what you like but justify it' exam title! I'd been messing about with cat pictures at the time, particularly snow leopards (how can any creature be that beautiful?) so the subject was sort of fixed. After some thought I remembered that incredible scene (for want of a better word) in 'The Magician's Nephew' when Aslan sings and all the animals are born out of the earth. So I decided to show one of those births. (I also decided that more dragon pictures might not be a good idea, as my tutors were getting rather sick of them...)

By the by - anyone thinking of doing a picture this size - don't use masonite (hardboard) as it weighs an absolute ton!

Before you is the Hall of the Healers, to your right is the Aisle of Cats, to your left is the Aisle of Dragons. Or you could return to the study.
