The Herb Garden

` The belly was even more finely
scaled than the arms,
almost silver in colour.`
- from Gibbon's Decline and Fall

You find yourself at the edge of a large open area, perhaps a huge lawn. It's set snug up to the northern cliff-wall and large geometric shapes have been sketched out with bamboo poles and string. It looks like someone's designing a garden.

To your left, a slim black dragon is sprawled cat-like on a woven rush mat, carefully placed in the warm sun. Noticing you, he puts down the book he's reading and nods at you in a friendly manner. As you venture forward, he rises gracefully to his feet and stands silently, letting you study him...

Rather to your surprise, he stands upright - on two legs - although his stance suggests he might be just as comfortable on four. Tall, he tops you by a head, maybe a little more. The powerful flight muscles and the huge wings they support, make his shoulders and neck seem broad and heavy, compared to narrow hips and sleek limbs and tail.

His skin - unscaled - is jet black and reflects the sunlight in a way that suggests short, velvety fur. Spines and crests decorate a narrow muzzle and a rather equine face. His eyes are large and green-gold, slit pupiled like a hunting cat. As you step closer, you can see he wears a large, heart-shaped ruby at his throat, sparkling on a golden chain. He (for so he obviously is) wears no clothing nor further decorations and seems rather amused by your attention.

Greetings neighbour. You must be the visitor Shia mentioned. Well, my greetings to you and welcome to my Lair, we're a little remote out here and it's nice to have visitors. Have you travelled far? If so, you`re more than welcome to rest here a while. I have more than space enough, although it's a bit rough. Better than camping out on the mountainside though, yes?

My name is Karne, and I'm master of this estate. That part which is presently in existence anyway.. as you can probably see I'm still building it! If you would like to stay a while, I'll take you to a guest room. Otherwise, please feel free to explore my gardens and I'll hope to see you again later.
