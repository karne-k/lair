Tetrogenic means that Taxol may cause deformed foetuses.

Chiral centres have two separate isomers, each being a mirror image of the other. The large number of chiral centres in Taxol, makes synthesis particularly difficult.

Mitosis is the process by which (non sex) cells undergo division into two daughter cells.

Organelles are smaller components within a cell, examples are: the nucleus, mitochondria and the endoplasmic reticulum.

The cytoplasm is the solution within the cell membrane, in which the organelles 'float'. Cytoplasm contains a very large number of dissolved salts, proteins and other macromolecules.

Range 400 meters (lethal at 60) and 5 arrows a minute.. Perhaps the most important medieval weapon.

Paclitaxel is the chemical name for the drug marketed as Taxol®. The supplier is Bristol-Myers-Squibb.

The chemical structure of Paclitaxel, consists of a 4 ring core with a amine sidegroup. The whole molecule is heavily oxygenated.

Docetaxel differs from Paclitaxel by two small functional groups, the core of the molecule is left unchanged.

Docetaxel is marketed as Taxotere® by Rhone-Poulene Rorer.

Noncytotoxic means that is does not have the same biological activity as Paclitaxel and Docetaxel.
