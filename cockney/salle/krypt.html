The Crypt
'...the words I need to explain what hurts...'

`simply aren't in my lexicon. `
 - Kathy
Curiosity wins over and you walk into the chamber. As you do so the tapestry falls into place, cutting you off. You walk into the centre of the golden room and look around. Stillness enfolds you and you find yourself unwilling to break it.

The mural shows a dragon - or a dragoness, you realise as you look a bit closer. She's reared upright - her slender form covering the entire wall, left foot on the floor, right arm reaching for the ceiling. Her wings stretch outwards, as if seeking to embrace the entire room - with you in it. In posture, she reminds you of a dancer or a gymnast - strong and supremely beautiful. Her expression is a smile and her eyes look outwards - not to you - but upwards to the sky. Behind her, the sun burns with a fierce intensity and you suddenly realise that she is not standing but flying - high up in the air above you. As you watch, she seems to twist and turn in the air - spinning before your confused eyes...

You stagger and with a jolt, return to reality. The mural is just a picture, flat on the wall and you are standing on solid rock, not flying above the clouds. With a soft hiss you shake your head to clear it and turn your attention somewhere less disturbing - the marble block.

This sits solidly before you, supporting candles, vase and flower. You delicately lift the blossom - a tiger lily and inhale it's fragrance. As you do so, you notice an engraving, cut deep into the surface of the marble. It says simply -

Flarebright
Perhaps you know this name and perhaps you don't but either way, you realise you trespass somewhere close to your host's heart. Gently, you replace the flower and turn to leave, as you do so, you feel a strange urge to nod to the mural and a mutter an soft apology for invading its peace.
