The Lair

`Delicious, just the right amount
of fat on him. And the poor boy
thought I was after his soul!`
- Sizzle

You head north towards the cliffs - It's a fair walk but worth it, for the dragon's lair turns out to be a huge temple-like structure, carved into the living rock. It has been built to take advantage of a series of limestone caverns that riddle the caldera wall like cracks in a cheese. Inside the Lair is opulent, even palatial both in size and decor.

You are directed through a series of loosely interlinking tunnels, some wide and high vaulted, others little more than cracks in the rock. There doesn't appear to be any fixed scheme to the Lair. In some areas, the walls have been left as bare rock, stalactites and stalagmites forming opalescent curtains against the torchlight. In others, the walls are of polished marble, panelled in wood or brightly painted with murals. It's all rather confusing but perhaps dragons like it that way...

The rooms you are shown are a bit cool but pleasingly comfortable. After settling in, eating and resting for a while, you are surprised to find that it is already late evening. At your request, a strangely feline aide directs you to The Master's Study where your host awaits you.
