Rorschach

`I am wat I am`

It's rather cold in the cave - the chill of ancient rock, long shaded from the sun. I shiver slightly and hurry to collect the fuel I need. Broken branches and brittle leaves of yesteryear, left to dry and shrivel in the darkness. Aged, the boughs will burn well - so I snap and stack them into a neat little pyramid. A touch and a soft spoke word to ignite the pile, and watch! - the darkness seems to whimper and cringe, scurrying away from the sudden golden flames. Soon afterwards, the cold follows, broken and dejected before such a heartless splendour.

I step back and settle down - relaxing - as the pleasant warmth spreads through me. Unconsciously, I purr - gently, softly - and watch the flickering of the fire with their strangely tortuous patterns. As I watch, leaves crisp and burn - brief lives incinerated in flame - coloured specks that dance bright and quietly die. Shapes and colourful patterns.

I watch, I wait. Eventually she arrives, her success obvious. A hart - flayed and cleaned - is dropped to the floor of the cave. It's head lolls foolishly, liquid eyes hazing over in death. In contrast, her's glow brightly in the reflected heat of the fire, the rush of the hunt. Laughing eyes - I smile to them.

She grins - blood on her muzzle - and I rise to help her lug the carcass over to the fire. Happy but tired, she slumps down and waves away my compliments - a good hunt, exciting and catch enough for both.

We eat and then - full and sleepy - rest together in pleasant companionship. Watching the fire, we talk - of this, of that - things of consequence and things of none. Finally, I ask her;

'What do you want from life?'

She thinks, then after a while, admits that she isn't very sure. Happiness perhaps? And security, a place to live, to hunt, a mate to love? And me? What would I want?

I chuckle softly and concede much the same - I don't really know either, I'm happy enough here, content and I wonder if it would be arrogant to ask for more?

She frowns slightly, then chuckles and promises not to tell anyone! I snort - that wasn't what I meant. Still ..

Would it be arrogant to ask for my dreams? My future, as I would have it? I fear that it might - but then - how can I not ask? Is it worse to remain silent or to speak out? And does it really make much difference?

I sigh softly and feel her eyes on me, she shifts herself around to watch me more comfortably.

'What do you want?'

I hesitate - 'My dream? It's the same as what it's always been - to spread my wings - to fly - to hunt. To live, to love and just to be.'

She digests this, then hisses softly, ' And would you give up what you have, for that?'

A hard question and rather a painful one, I've been unable to decide on the answer, 'I might,' I reply, 'I might, but I don't really know...'
